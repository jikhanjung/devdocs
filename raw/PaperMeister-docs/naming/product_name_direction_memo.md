# Product Name Direction Memo

**Date:** 2026-04-03  
**Status:** Draft  
**Scope:** Reconsidering the product name that currently appears as `PaperMeister`, assuming the parent company brand is `Noematica`

## 1. Purpose

The company name and the product name do not need to solve the same problem.

If `Noematica` becomes the company brand, the product name should be evaluated separately.

The purpose of this memo is to answer:

- why `PaperMeister` may no longer fit the broader direction
- what kind of product name would fit better
- whether names like `NoomaticEngine` or `Nooshelf` are better aligned

## 2. Why `PaperMeister` Feels Increasingly Limited

`PaperMeister` worked as an early-stage name because it clearly suggested:

- papers
- handling or mastery
- a useful desktop-style tool

But the product direction has widened.

It is no longer just:

- a paper organizer
- a PDF utility
- a paper-search tool

It is becoming closer to:

- a research corpus layer
- a multi-source literature system
- a searchable local archive
- the entry point into a larger knowledge infrastructure

So the limitations of `PaperMeister` are becoming more visible.

### Main issues

- `paper` feels too narrow and document-bound
- `meister` feels a little old-fashioned or utility-like
- the tone fits a tool, but less a serious long-term product platform
- it does not naturally align with `Noematica` as a company brand

## 3. What the New Product Name Should Do

A replacement product name should ideally:

- feel more modern than `PaperMeister`
- still suggest literature, archive, or library use
- fit under `Noematica` naturally
- remain product-like rather than too philosophical
- avoid sounding too much like an internal engine or framework

In short:

The product name should feel more tangible and user-facing than the company name.

## 4. Why `NoematicEngine` Is Not Ideal as the Main Product Name

`NoematicEngine` is not a bad name, but it likely fits better as:

- an internal platform name
- a technical engine
- a runtime or architecture component

rather than the main user-facing product.

### Strengths

- conceptually aligned with `Noematica`
- infrastructure and systems feeling
- serious and technical

### Weaknesses

- too abstract for many users
- feels more like a backend or platform component
- weaker as the name of the product people directly install and use

Conclusion:

`NoematicEngine` is more plausible as an engine name than as the replacement for `PaperMeister`.

## 5. Product Naming Directions

The most relevant directions seem to be:

- archive / library / shelf names
- index / browse / sync names
- product names that combine a light conceptual prefix with a tangible object

## 6. Strong Candidate: `Nooshelf`

`Nooshelf` is currently one of the strongest product-name candidates.

## Why It Works

- `Noo-` keeps a light connection to the broader knowledge / noos / noema family
- `shelf` gives an immediately understandable sense of stored and reusable literature
- it sounds more like a real product than `NoematicEngine`
- it is less old than `PaperMeister`
- it is less NLP-coded than `Corpus...`
- it fits naturally under `Noematica`

## What It Suggests

`Nooshelf` suggests:

- a place where literature is organized
- a structured shelf of knowledge
- stored material that can be searched and reused

This is surprisingly close to the actual MVP:

- gather papers
- OCR them
- search them
- keep them reusable as a local corpus

## Risks

- `shelf` may sound slightly more like organization/storage than infrastructure
- the `Noo-` prefix may still need explanation
- Korean pronunciation is workable but not perfectly natural

Even so, the balance is strong.

## 7. Korean-Language Feel

Possible Korean feel:

- `누셸프`
- `노오셸프`

Neither is perfect, but the name still behaves more like a usable product name than many abstract options.

It is less awkward in practical product use than:

- `Noosphere`
- `Nooscape`
- `NoematicEngine`

## 8. How It Works with the Company Brand

The combination is strong:

- **Company:** Noematica
- **Product:** Nooshelf

This creates a useful separation:

- `Noematica` carries the broader philosophy and infrastructure identity
- `Nooshelf` carries the concrete user-facing product identity

Example usage:

- `Nooshelf by Noematica`
- `Noematica presents Nooshelf`
- `Nooshelf is a local research corpus tool from Noematica`

## 9. Other Product-Name Candidates Worth Keeping

Even if `Nooshelf` is strong, a few nearby directions remain worth comparing.

### Archive / Library Direction

- Noematica Archive
- Noematica Library
- ArchiveLayer
- LibraryLayer

These are clearer, but more generic and less brand-like.

### Index / Discovery Direction

- Noematica Index
- Noema Index
- IndexLayer

These fit search and retrieval, but may underplay archive/corpus-building.

### `Noo-` Product Direction

- Nooshelf
- Noostack
- Noosync
- Noostore

Among these, `Nooshelf` currently feels the most balanced and human.

## 10. Why `Nooshelf` May Be Better Than `PaperMeister`

Compared with `PaperMeister`, `Nooshelf` feels:

- more contemporary
- less utility-like
- less narrowly “paper” focused
- more naturally connected to a broader company brand

It still keeps enough warmth and usability to sound like a real tool people would use.

## 11. Positioning Sentences Using `Nooshelf`

### English

- `Nooshelf is a local research corpus tool for turning scattered literature into a searchable archive.`
- `Nooshelf helps researchers turn PDF collections, Zotero libraries, and archive folders into a living corpus.`
- `Nooshelf is the literature layer of the Noematica stack.`

### Korean

- `Nooshelf는 흩어진 문헌을 searchable archive로 바꾸는 로컬 연구 코퍼스 도구입니다.`
- `Nooshelf는 PDF 폴더와 Zotero 라이브러리를 살아 있는 문헌 코퍼스로 바꿉니다.`
- `Nooshelf는 Noematica 스택의 문헌 레이어입니다.`

## 12. Current Recommendation

If the company brand is `Noematica`, then the current most plausible replacement direction for `PaperMeister` is:

**Nooshelf**

because it achieves a useful balance between:

- concept
- usability
- product-ness
- brand compatibility

## 13. Summary

The product name should not try to do the same work as the company name.

If `Noematica` is the broader, more conceptual company brand, then the product needs a more grounded and user-facing identity.

`NoematicEngine` is likely better as a technical platform name.

`Nooshelf` is stronger as the user-facing product name because it feels:

- more installable
- more memorable
- more concrete
- and more naturally aligned with the actual MVP experience

At the current stage, `Nooshelf` is one of the strongest candidates for replacing `PaperMeister`.
