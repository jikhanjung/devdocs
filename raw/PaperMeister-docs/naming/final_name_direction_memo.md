# Final Name Direction Memo

**Date:** 2026-04-03  
**Status:** Draft  
**Scope:** Narrowing the naming direction based on semantic fit, tone, and Korean-language feel

## 1. Purpose

This memo summarizes the naming direction after exploring multiple candidate families.

The goal is not to produce a final answer immediately.
The goal is to identify:

- which naming directions are no longer a fit
- which semantic and tonal criteria matter most
- which remaining candidates best fit the current company identity

## 2. What the Company Name Needs to Do

The company is no longer best understood as:

- a paleontology-only company
- a generic AI app company
- a narrow OCR or text-mining company

It is moving toward something closer to:

- scholarly engineering
- research infrastructure
- knowledge systems
- corpus and evidence infrastructure
- packaging and distribution of reusable scholarly artifacts

So the company name must be broad enough to hold:

- PaperMeister
- SCODA
- PaleoBase
- future domain packages

## 3. Naming Directions That No Longer Fit Well

Several naming families were useful for exploration, but now appear weaker in practice.

## 3.1 `Paleo...`

Why it weakens:

- locks the company too early into paleontology
- makes it harder to present a broader infrastructure vision
- works better for domain packages than for the parent company

Conclusion:

Keep `Paleo...` for domain-layer naming, not company naming.

## 3.2 `Scholar...`

Example:

- ScholarForge

Why it weakens:

- semantically strong
- but sounds somewhat traditional or old-fashioned
- feels closer to academic identity than infrastructure identity

Conclusion:

Good in meaning, weaker in tone.

## 3.3 `Corpus...`

Example:

- CorpusForge

Why it weakens:

- technically accurate
- but too easily coded as linguistics, NLP, or text processing
- narrower than the actual company vision

Conclusion:

Too text-centric for the broader infrastructure direction.

## 3.4 `Evidence...`

Example:

- Evidentia

Why it weakens:

- fits evidence-first philosophy
- but sounds closer to legal, medical, forensic, or proof-oriented fields
- does not naturally suggest building systems or shared knowledge infrastructure

Conclusion:

Philosophically aligned, but tonally off.

## 3.5 `Strata...`

Example:

- Strataform

Why it weakens:

- suggests layers and structure, which is conceptually relevant
- but also leaks geological or stratigraphic associations
- `form` feels less clearly motivated in the compound
- may sound more domain-specific than intended

Conclusion:

Structurally interesting, but semantically not fully convincing.

## 4. What the Preferred Name Now Needs to Feel Like

The desired company name now seems to require:

- broad knowledge / infrastructure scope
- not domain-locked
- not too old academic
- not too NLP/text-mining coded
- not too legal/medical/forensic
- not too geology-specific
- some sense of building, runtime, or generative infrastructure
- reasonably workable in Korean pronunciation as well

In short:

The preferred name should feel like a living knowledge systems company, not a narrow tool or a traditional academic institution.

## 5. Why the `Noe / Noos` Direction Survives

The strongest remaining family appears to be the `Noe / Noos` direction.

This family works because it:

- escapes paleontology lock-in
- escapes NLP-coded “corpus” framing
- escapes the older feel of “scholar”
- retains philosophical and knowledge-oriented depth
- can still support an infrastructure identity if paired with the right suffix

It is abstract, but not empty.

## 6. Remaining Core Candidates

The strongest remaining candidates appear to be:

- NoemaEngine
- NoemaForge
- NoosForge

with `Noemata` remaining a possible secondary option.

## 7. Candidate Analysis

## 7.1 NoemaEngine

### Strengths

- sounds like a systems company
- supports infrastructure, runtime, and platform identity
- broad enough to contain multiple products
- Korean rendering `노에마엔진` is relatively stable

### Risks

- slightly functional and technical
- can sound more like a product or engine than a company
- less emotionally distinctive than the `Forge` variants

### Overall

Best if the priority is:

- stability
- infrastructure identity
- clarity of systems orientation

## 7.2 NoemaForge

### Strengths

- combines conceptual depth with a sense of building
- more alive and generative than `NoemaEngine`
- avoids the old-fashioned feel of `ScholarForge`
- more distinctive than many descriptive names

### Risks

- more abstract than `NoemaEngine`
- `Forge` may require some explanation outside technical audiences
- Korean `노에마포지` is distinctive but slightly unfamiliar

### Overall

Best if the priority is:

- brand character
- conceptual depth
- building/creation energy

## 7.3 NoosForge

### Strengths

- shorter and sharper than `NoemaForge`
- strongest modern brand feeling among the current shortlist
- keeps the knowledge/philosophical root without sounding too academic
- less old than `ScholarForge`
- less text-coded than `CorpusForge`

### Risks

- `Noos` is less immediately interpretable than `Noema`
- Korean rendering can vary (`누스포지`, `노오스포지`)
- meaning may require explanation in early use

### Overall

Best if the priority is:

- compact brand identity
- modern tone
- memorable distinctiveness

## 7.4 Noemata

### Strengths

- elegant and intellectual
- conceptually rich
- Korean `노에마타` is relatively natural

### Risks

- highly abstract
- less obviously infrastructural
- less active than `Engine` or `Forge`

### Overall

Strong as a philosophical brand, weaker as an immediately practical company identity.

## 8. Korean-Language Considerations

Korean pronunciation matters because the name must work in real conversation.

### NoemaEngine

- `노에마엔진`
- clear enough
- practical and stable

### NoemaForge

- `노에마포지`
- memorable, but more foreign-feeling

### NoosForge

- `누스포지` or `노오스포지`
- more brand-like, but less stable in rendering

### Noemata

- `노에마타`
- smooth as a spoken proper noun

This suggests:

- `Noema...` names are easier in Korean than `Noos...` names
- `NoosForge` may be strongest in English brand feel, but weaker in Korean stability

## 9. Current Decision Shape

The naming decision now appears to be between:

### Stability / infrastructure path

- NoemaEngine

### Conceptual / brand path

- NoemaForge

### Compact / modern brand path

- NoosForge

## 10. Practical Recommendation

If choosing based on the current discussion alone:

- choose `NoemaEngine` if systems identity matters most
- choose `NoemaForge` if conceptual strength plus builder energy matters most
- choose `NoosForge` if brand sharpness and modernity matter most

## 11. Summary

Several earlier candidates were valuable for discovery, but now appear weaker:

- `Paleo...` is too narrow
- `Scholar...` feels too old
- `Corpus...` feels too NLP-oriented
- `Evidentia` feels too legal/medical
- `Strataform` feels too geologic and semantically uneven

The strongest surviving direction is now:

- `Noema...`
- `Noos...`

with the practical top tier being:

- NoemaEngine
- NoemaForge
- NoosForge

These names better match the company’s likely identity as a broader scholarly infrastructure / knowledge systems company.
