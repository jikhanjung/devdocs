# Product Name Shortlist Refresh

**Date:** 2026-04-03  
**Status:** Draft  
**Scope:** Updated shortlist for replacing `PaperMeister`, with `Noostrata` added as a stronger candidate

## 1. Why This Refresh Is Needed

The earlier product-name direction memo identified `Nooshelf` as a strong candidate, mainly because it felt more product-like than `NoematicEngine`.

However, further reflection exposed a real limitation:

- `shelf` feels too small
- too storage-like
- too static
- and not fully aligned with the product’s direction as a searchable, layered corpus system

That makes an updated shortlist necessary.

## 2. What the Product Name Needs to Convey

The product is no longer just:

- a paper organizer
- a PDF utility
- a personal shelf

It is moving toward:

- a local research corpus layer
- a searchable and continuously updated literature system
- a bridge from documents to structured scholarly knowledge

So the product name should feel:

- more layered than `shelf`
- more user-facing than `engine`
- more serious than `PaperMeister`
- more product-like than a generic platform component

## 3. Current Shortlist

The most relevant current candidates are:

- Noostrata
- Nooshelf
- NoematicEngine
- Noematica Archive

## 4. Candidate Review

## 4.1 Noostrata

### Why it works

- `Noo-` keeps the knowledge / noos / noema connection
- `strata` suggests layers, accumulation, and structure
- this matches the product’s movement from documents to layered corpora and structured knowledge
- stronger and broader than `shelf`
- more product-like than `NoematicEngine`

### What it suggests

- layered knowledge
- accumulated research structure
- multi-layer scholarly corpus

### Strengths

- more serious and substantial than `Nooshelf`
- better aligned with corpus infrastructure
- still distinctive and brand-like
- works naturally with `Noematica`

### Risks

- `strata` can still evoke geology or stratigraphy
- slightly heavier in tone than simpler names
- Korean rendering is workable but not especially light

### Overall

One of the strongest current candidates, especially if the product is meant to feel more like a layered corpus system than a simple literature organizer.

## 4.2 Nooshelf

### Why it works

- immediate shelf / literature storage intuition
- warm and product-like
- easier to imagine as something a user installs

### Weakness

- too much storage and organization feeling
- too small for the current product ambition
- less suitable if the product becomes more corpus- and knowledge-layer oriented

### Overall

Still a decent product name, but now feels weaker than `Noostrata`.

## 4.3 NoematicEngine

### Why it works

- infrastructure and runtime feel
- technically serious
- aligned with the company’s conceptual direction

### Weakness

- too much like an internal engine or backend component
- not ideal as the main user-facing product name
- weak warmth and usability tone

### Overall

Better as an internal platform name than the flagship product name.

## 4.4 Noematica Archive

### Why it works

- clear and understandable
- fits naturally under the company brand
- communicates archive function directly

### Weakness

- generic
- less distinctive
- more descriptive than brand-like
- does not feel as memorable as a standalone product name

### Overall

A safe fallback name, but not the strongest brand candidate.

## 5. Comparative Summary

## Noostrata

- best balance of structure, depth, and product identity

## Nooshelf

- most approachable, but too small in scope

## NoematicEngine

- strongest as a technical platform or internal engine

## Noematica Archive

- clearest, but most generic

## 6. Korean-Language Feel

### Noostrata

- `누스트라타` / `노오스트라타`
- heavier but still usable
- sounds more serious than `Nooshelf`

### Nooshelf

- `누셸프` / `노오셸프`
- lighter, but less stable and slightly awkward

### NoematicEngine

- `노에마틱엔진`
- clear enough, but very technical

### Noematica Archive

- `노에마티카 아카이브`
- natural enough, but not especially elegant as a product label

## 7. How the Product and Company Pairing Feels

### Noematica + Noostrata

- strongest sense of a layered scholarly system
- feels like a serious product family

### Noematica + Nooshelf

- friendlier and lighter
- more app-like

### Noematica + NoematicEngine

- more platform stack than product family

### Noematica + Noematica Archive

- coherent but less memorable

## 8. Current Recommendation

At the current stage, the most promising direction appears to be:

**Company:** Noematica  
**Product:** Noostrata

This combination works because:

- the company keeps the broader scholarly infrastructure identity
- the product gains a stronger, more layered identity than `PaperMeister`
- the name suggests corpus depth rather than simple storage

## 10. Summary

The updated shortlist makes one thing clearer:

- `Nooshelf` is good, but too shelf-like
- `NoematicEngine` is useful, but too engine-like
- `Noematica Archive` is safe, but too generic

`Noostrata` currently offers the strongest balance of:

- conceptual depth
- product identity
- structural fit
- and long-term compatibility with the broader product direction

For now, it is the leading candidate for replacing `PaperMeister`.
