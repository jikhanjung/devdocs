# PaperMeister Company Name Brainstorm

**Date:** 2026-04-03  
**Status:** Draft  
**Scope:** Broad company naming exploration for a domain-neutral research infrastructure / scholarly engineering company

## 1. Naming Context

The company should not be locked too early into a single domain such as paleontology.

Although the current work has strong paleontology roots, the broader direction is now closer to:

- scholarly engineering
- corpus infrastructure
- research knowledge systems
- evidence-first data packaging
- reusable academic infrastructure

That means the company name should be broader than:

- `Paleo...`
- a single database project
- a single product such as PaperMeister

## 2. Naming Goals

The name should ideally:

- avoid hard-locking the company to paleontology
- feel compatible with scholarly / infrastructure / knowledge work
- be able to contain products like PaperMeister, SCODA, and PaleoBase
- avoid generic AI hype language
- sound credible as a serious long-term systems company

## 3. Naming Directions

The current naming space seems to fall into several useful categories:

- scholarly / corpus / knowledge oriented
- infrastructure / systems / runtime oriented
- archive / memory / continuity oriented
- more brand-like, less descriptive coined names

## 9. Stronger Shortlist Candidates

Based on the current direction, the following feel especially promising.

## 9.1 ScholarForge

Why it works:

- matches the “scholarly engineering” idea
- sounds active and technical
- broad enough for multiple products

Risk:

- slightly direct and explanation-heavy

## 9.2 CorpusFoundry

Why it works:

- fits PaperMeister and SCODA very well
- implies making structured assets from raw material
- clearly aligned with corpus infrastructure

Risk:

- somewhat descriptive

## 9.3 Evidentia

Why it works:

- strong alignment with evidence-first philosophy
- credible and brand-like
- broad enough to scale beyond one product

Risk:

- less immediately obvious in function

## 9.4 Strataform

Why it works:

- suggests layers, structure, formation
- works surprisingly well for both infrastructure and scholarly systems
- subtly resonates with paleontology roots without locking into it

Risk:

- slightly abstract

## 9.5 Groundlayer

Why it works:

- sounds like infrastructure
- implies foundational layer beneath applications
- fits the “corpus OS” idea

Risk:

- more technical than scholarly

## 9.6 Scholarium

Why it works:

- scholarly and institutional feeling
- elegant tone
- broad enough to support multiple products

Risk:

- softer and less engineering-forward

## 9.7 Infraloom

Why it works:

- suggests weaving distributed materials together
- strong infrastructure undertone
- distinctive brand character

Risk:

- unusual word may need explanation

## 9.8 Structura

Why it works:

- emphasizes structure and systems
- concise and credible
- fits corpus, knowledge, and packaging layers

Risk:

- somewhat generic

## 10. Suggested Evaluation Axes

To narrow the list, evaluate each name on:

- domain neutrality
- scholarly credibility
- infrastructure feel
- distinctiveness
- ease of saying and remembering
- compatibility with PaperMeister / SCODA / PaleoBase product family

## 11. Practical Recommendation

At this stage, it is better to choose between these two strategic directions first:

### Direction A: Descriptive and clear

Examples:

- ScholarForge
- CorpusFoundry
- Scholarium
- Structura

### Direction B: Brand-like and expandable

Examples:

- Evidentia
- Strataform
- Infraloom
- Veridion

If early trust and clarity matter most, Direction A is safer.
If long-term branding matters more, Direction B may age better.

## 13. Summary

The naming direction should stay broader than paleontology.

The company is increasingly positioned as something closer to:

- scholarly engineering
- corpus infrastructure
- evidence-based knowledge systems
- research infrastructure

So the company name should reflect that wider ambition.

At the moment, the best balance appears to be between:

- a clear, engineering-forward name such as `ScholarForge` or `CorpusFoundry`
- and a stronger long-term brand name such as `Evidentia` or `Strataform`
