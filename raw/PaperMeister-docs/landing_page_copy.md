## Problem

Your papers are not really in one place.

Some are in Zotero. Some are still in EndNote. Important PDFs live in folders, external drives, or old scan archives. You know the literature is somewhere in your system, but your actual research corpus is fragmented, half-searchable, and hard to inherit.

Reference managers manage references. File folders store files. Desktop search finds strings.

None of them turn your real paper archive into a usable research corpus.

## Solution

PaperMeister sits above your existing tools and turns them into one living corpus.

It does not ask you to abandon your current workflow.

Instead, it:

- ingests papers from multiple sources
- OCRs PDFs so scans and hard-to-search files become searchable
- preserves text and source provenance
- keeps building an internal corpus layer you can search and extend

## Why Now

Recent OCR and layout-analysis quality make this possible in a way it often was not before.

PDFs are no longer just attachments. They can become readable, searchable corpus assets.

That changes the problem from “where did I put this paper?” to “how do I operate my whole literature base as a reusable research asset?”

## Key Benefits

### Use the tools you already have

Zotero, EndNote-style records, and directory-based PDF archives can all feed into the same corpus workflow.

### Search what was previously dead storage

Scanned papers and archived PDFs become searchable once OCR is applied.

### Keep provenance visible

PaperMeister does not flatten everything blindly. It keeps track of where records and files came from.

### Build more than a library

The goal is not only search. The long-term result is a structured corpus that can support analysis, extraction, and research assistance.

## FAQ

### Do I need to replace Zotero or EndNote?

No. PaperMeister is designed to sit above existing tools, not replace them.

### Is this cloud-only?

No. The product is being designed around local corpus ownership and controlled sync with external sources.

### Is this only for papers with good text layers?

No. A major purpose is to make OCR-backed search possible for difficult PDFs and scans.

### Is this just a PDF search app?

No. Search is the first layer. The broader goal is a structured, reusable research corpus.
